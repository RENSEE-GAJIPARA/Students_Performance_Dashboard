----------------------------------------------------------
5. ATTENDANCE ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------------------------
- Overall attendance rate is strong at 90.05% (90,048
  Present vs 9,952 Absent records out of 1,00,000 logs).
- Absences are fairly evenly distributed in terms of reason
  (no single dominant cause), suggesting absenteeism is
  driven by varied, individual circumstances rather than one
  systemic issue.
- Surprisingly, attendance % and average score % show almost
  NO meaningful correlation (correlation coefficient ~ -0.05)
  at the student level. This means simply attending classes
  is not, by itself, translating into better scores -
  pointing toward a need to focus on in-class engagement and
  teaching quality rather than attendance alone.

----------------------------------------------------------
6. BEHAVIORAL INSIGHTS
----------------------------------------------------------
- Behavior records are nearly evenly split across 5 types:
    Disruptive               -> 1,333 records (~20.5%)
    Late                     -> 1,302 records (~20.0%)
    Helpful                  -> 1,299 records (~20.0%)
    Participative            -> 1,294 records (~19.9%)
    Absent without notice    -> 1,272 records (~19.6%)
- "Disruptive" behavior is the single most frequently logged
  behavior type, marginally ahead of the others - worth
  monitoring at the classroom level.
- Positive behaviors (Helpful + Participative) together
  account for roughly 40% of all logged behavior, balancing
  out the negative behavior types (Disruptive, Late, Absent
  without notice) at ~60%.

----------------------------------------------------------
7. STUDENT DEMOGRAPHICS
----------------------------------------------------------
- Gender split: 525 Male (52.5%) vs 475 Female (47.5%)
  students - a reasonably balanced cohort.

----------------------------------------------------------
8. RECOMMENDATIONS
----------------------------------------------------------
1. Launch targeted remedial sessions for Class 7 (History),
   Class 12 (History), and Class 6/8 (Geography) - the
   weakest class-subject combinations.
2. Investigate root causes of "Disruptive" and "Late"
   behavior patterns, as these are the most frequently
   logged negative behaviors.
3. Since attendance alone does not strongly predict scores,
   shift focus toward improving engagement quality, teaching
   methods, and assessment design rather than attendance
   enforcement alone.
4. Build on Term 3's improved performance by identifying
   what changed (teaching methods, revision schedules, etc.)
   and replicate it in Terms 1 and 2.
5. Since ~40% of records fall in the "Low" performance band,
   consider introducing a structured tutoring/mentorship
   program for at-risk students flagged via the
   Performance Category measure.
